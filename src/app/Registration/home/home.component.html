FRATERNITY IN BRIEF

The concept of Fraternity funds is indigenous to India and originated more than 1000 years ago. Initially it was in the form of an informal association of traders and households within communities, wherein the members contributed some money in return for an accumulated sum at the end of the tenure.
It is a mechanism that combines borrowing and savings in a single scheme. In a Fraternity fund scheme , a group of individuals come together for a pre-determined time and contribute to a common pool at regular intervals. Every month, up until the end of the tenure of the scheme, the collected pool of money is loaned out internally through a bidding mechanism to the most deserving member. This way, people who need funds and those who want to save are able to meet their requirements.
Fraternity funds are equivalent of the Rotating Savings and Credit Associations (ROSCA) that are famous throughout the world. ROSCAs are a means to "save and borrow" simultaneously. It is considered one of the best instruments to cater to the needs of a customer. It enables people to convert their small savings into lump sums.

How does Fraternity fund work?

The following illustration will explain how a Fraternity fund works.
There is a new Fraternity fund group that is commencing. The value of the Fraternity fund is 2,00,000. This amount is known as the Fraternity fund Value.

For this illustration, we will consider the duration of the Fraternity fund to be 20 months. This is known as term period for the Fraternity fund group.
The Foreman or Fraternity fund Company must gather 20 people who are interested in investing in this Fraternity fund group. These 20 persons are called Subscribers.
Once the Fraternity fund group commences, the foreman must register the Fraternity fund with the registrar of Fraternity funds. He must pay 100% of the Fraternity fund value as security.
This amount can be withdrawn only after the said Fraternity fund group closes and every subscriber is paid what is due to him or her. This regulation protects the interests of the subscriber to a certain extent.
The amount collected from the members in the group is called the pot.

The amount to be saved every month by the Subscriber is Fraternity fund value/No of person’s i.e. 2,00,000/20 = 10,000.
This is also known as Monthly Subscription.
It may be noted here that the number of persons investing in a Fraternity fund and the duration of the Fraternity fund is always the same.
The maximum monthly contribution or Subscription Amount that a Subscriber will pay is 10,000.
The Subscriber does not always pay the entire Subscription amount every month.
Let us find out how and why he does not have to pay the entire amount.

The maximum discount or maximum bid permitted ranges from 5-35%. For this illustration we will assume that a Subscriber has bid 35% of the Fraternity fund value. 35% of 2,00,000 is 1,30,000. This amount is known as Fraternity fund Discount.

The foreman charges 5% of Fraternity fund value as commission. In this illustration, that would amount to 5,000. This is deducted from Fraternity fund Discount i.e. 70,000-5000=65,000. This amount is available for equal distribution among the subscribers.
In our example, 65,000 would be divided equally among 20 subscribers. Each subscriber would get a discount of 65,000/20 = 3250. This amount is known as Dividend.

In the following month, all subscribers would have to only pay 3,250 instead of 10,000. This is because they have earned a Fraternity fund discount of 3250.
Depending upon the competition within the Fraternity fund group the discount percent would vary. The least discount would be 5% which is the foreman’s commission. The bidding process will continue until the end of the Fraternity fund period.
In this manner, the subscribers can borrow more than what they paid in the first half period of the Fraternity fund term and save their money if they decide to wait further into the Fraternity fund period.
