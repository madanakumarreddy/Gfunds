Frequently Asked Questions

1. How safe is my money with Fraternity Funds?

Safety of one’s money in the hands of another person depends on the honesty and integrity of the person managing your funds. Many Fraternity Funds with working history of over 8 years reveals that they have not defaulted in repayment of any person’s money.

2. Why should I join Fraternity Funds?

Look for a professionally managed company in the service of its subscribers with a track record. Look for the group is socially responsible. For example, supports a non -profit cancer hospital and provides scholarships to poor meritorious students etc. Some Fraternity Funds in the interest of its subscribers offers a discount of 0.5% of the installment, if the installments are paid before the 10th of every month. There is also a provision for payment at any of their Branch or collection of the monthly installments from your doorstep. They also have plans for online payment.

3. How does a Fraternity fund a better saving option, when compared to a deposit in a Bank, Mutual fund etc.?

By joining a Fraternity fund, a person is forced to save a small part of his income, to meet his planned/unplanned investments or expenditure. In case of contingency, borrowing from the Fraternity fund is much easier, simpler, faster and cheaper than borrowing from a bank. The Fraternity loss is normally lower than the market rate of interest. In case a person wants to save till the last, the return a subscriber gets by way of Fraternity dividends is much higher than interest from banks on recurring deposits.

4. Who is a prized subscriber?

A subscriber who has lifted his Fraternity or to whom the Fraternity amount is paid is called a prized subscriberscriber.

5. Who is a non-prized subscriber?

A subscriber who has not lifted his Fraternity or to whom the Fraternity amount has not been paid yet is called a non-prized subscriber.

6. What are the benefits?

Persons who want to save money and receive the prized/bid amount in the second half of the Fraternity period, earn very much higher return than their periodic savings kept with a bank. Those in need of money can raise money by receiving the prized/bid amount when they need money. The interest or Fraternity loss in such cases is very much less than the market rates. All persons joining a Fraternity group are forced to save a small amount of their income to finance their dream projects such as – purchasing a plot or building a house or meeting marriage expenses or buying domestic articles, or discharging an existing liability etc.
Each person who pays his periodic installments up to date is entitled to receive the prized amount at a period of his choice, after participating in a lottery/auction.

7. What are the benefits of receiving Fraternity amount in the first few months, at a discount or loss?

Since the successful bidder, in the initial few months can use the Fraternity amount, for his personal use ahead of others, he offers to take the Fraternity amount at a discount, after calculation interest and cost of funds. It may be noted that a successful bidder withdraws more money from the company, than he had already paid, and repays the excess amount withdrawn, in periodic installments until completion of the Fraternity period. The excess amount so paid is known as Fraternity loss.

8. What is auction discount?

The difference between Fraternity value and the amount at which a successful bidder takes a Fraternity in a lottery auction is known as auction discount.

9. What is an auction?

Auction is a procedure for identification of the non-prized subscriber who wants to take a Fraternity amount at the highest permissible discount. All non-prized subscribers who have paid their installments up to date can participate in the auction for bidding up to the highest auction discount, within a period of five minutes allowed for each auction.

10. What is Fraternity dividend?

Auction discount minus company commission (5% of Fraternity value) is the total group dividend. Total group dividend is distributed equally amongst all the subscribers. This dividend so distributed is deducted from the next installment payable by the subscriber.

11. What is lot?

When more than one non-prized subscriber wants to take the Fraternity amount at the same discount, the Fraternity numbers (coins) of all such non-prized subscribers are put in a container and the successful bidder is identified by removing one number (token coin) from the said container. The procedure is known as lot or lottery.

12. What is “coming to open”?

When there is no member in the Fraternity group who is willing to receive the prized/bid amount at the maximum allowable discount, the bidding for the Fraternity comes in for open auction. This is known as coming to open.

13. How many sureties or guarantors are required?

Normally for all Fraternity’s a minimum of three sureties or guarantors other than family members are insisted upon. The basic salary or taxable income of all such sureties or guarantors should not be less than 20% of the Fraternity’s’ future liability.

14. What are the benefits to the Foreman/Fraternity Fund Company?

The Fraternity fund company conducting the Fraternity business also known as Foreman charges 5% of the Fraternity value as commission or service charge for the services rendered by it. The foreman must gather a group of persons to form a Fraternity group, collecting money from each subscriber every month, arranging payment of prized money to one person each month and collecting money from prized subscribers etc. is work for the foreman.

15. What regulations does the Fraternity fund company follow?

All activities of the company are regulated/monitored by the government department under the Fraternity fund Act 1982.

16. Why are sureties or guarantors taken?

A Fraternity fund company undertakes to collect periodic installments from all the subscribers and pay the amount so collected, after deduction its commission (5% or the Fraternity value) to one of the non-prized subscriber each month. At every point in time, the Fraternity fund company pays the prized subscriber more money, than what it had collected from the prized subscriber, on the understanding that the prized subscriber will pay the balance future installment on time. In other words, the prized subscriber takes a loan advance from the Fraternity fund company, with an understanding to repay the balance future installment in time, to enable the foreman to pay the prized money to other non-prized subscribers each month. To safe guard the interest of all non-prized subscribers each month the Fraternity fund company insists upon the prized subscriber to provide guarantee for repayment of future installments.

17. What action will be taken against defaulting subscribers?

Penalty is collected from all subscribers who make delayed payment of periodic installments. In case a non-prized subscriber defaults for a continuous period of three months, such subscriber will be removed. The actual amount paid by him in the Fraternity minus company commission will be repaid to such defaulting subscriber, after completion of the Fraternity period. In case a prized subscriber defaults for a continuous period of three months, a penalty of 6% per month will be collected and dividends will also not be paid to such defaulting prized subscriber. Legal action will be initiated against the said defaulting prized subscriber and his guarantors in court of law, for recovery of all dues.

18. Can I withdraw from a Fraternity group after becoming a member?

When a subscriber withdraws, the foreman must find an alternate subscriber in his place. Until then the Fraternity fund company must arrange funds on behalf of such a defaulting subscriber, for payment to a prized subscriber each month. Hence withdrawal is permitted, but the actual amount paid by the subscriber in the Fraternity minus company commission will be repaid to such a defaulting subscriber at the end of the Fraternity period.

19. How soon can I expect payment once I bid for the Fraternity?

Payment can be expected after completion of documentation.

20. What are the income tax benefits by saving and borrowing using Fraternity’s?

The Fraternity loss can be deducted from the income from profession or business. The net surplus or dividends after completion of the Fraternity period need to be offered as income, but some courts have ruled that this income is not subject to income tax.

21. What are the modes of payment?

Cheques, demand draft, pay order and bank transfer are accepted modes of payment.

22. How many Fraternities can I take in a Fraternity group?

A subscriber can participate more than one Fraternity in the same Fraternity group, but the no. of Fraternity’s allowed will be at the discretion of the foreman.

23. What it the duration of these Fraternity’s?

The duration of Fraternity’s are normally 18 months and 20 months.

24. Can we make payments in other branches?

Yes, many Fraternity’s funds allow you to make payments at any of their branch.

25. What are the benefits of joining a vacant Fraternity?

All the dividends receivable till date of joining the group are earned by the member joining a vacant Fraternity.

26. Registered and Unregistered Fraternity fund companies?

Registered Fraternity funds are regulated by the government departments, Unregistered Fraternity funds are prohibited by the government and the subscriber will be at the mercy of the promoter without support from government agencies.
